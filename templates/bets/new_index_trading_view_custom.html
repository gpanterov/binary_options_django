What are binary options?

Binary options can be tought of as a "bet" on whether the price of a financial asset will end up above or below a certain threshold. The threshold, which the price needs to cross is called a strike price . The payout of a binary options is always the same multiple regardless of by how much or how little the strike price was crossed.

Call option is successful if the price of the asset is above the strike when the option expires. On the other hand, a Put option is successful if the price is below the strike price at expiration.

With the simple binary options, the strike price is the current market price at the time the option was purchased and the payout is always the same - 91% on top of the initial amount. Please see Custom binary options for setting an arbitrary strike price and getting different payouts.
